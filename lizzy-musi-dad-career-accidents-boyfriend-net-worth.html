Professional Career: Who Is Lizzy Musi?

Well, you know what they say, ‘the apple does not fall far from the tree.’ True to the statement, Lizzy coming from a family of professional car racers, chose to follow the tradition herself. It came as no surprise when Lizzy decided to pursue her career in drag racing.

Similarly, her whole family, including her mother and sisters, are also professional drag racer who supported her decision wholeheartedly. Not to mention, it was her father, Pat, who greatly influenced her love for the sport and encouraged her to turn professional.

And what do you know, the girl’s got the genes from her father. Lizzy, upon her entry, created a big buzz with her talent. Moreover, Musi’s status increased after winning her career first National Level Pro Nitrous at the PDRA at Virginia Motorsports Park in Dinwiddie, VA.

Furthermore, in 2014, Lizzy became the first woman to break 200 MPH in eighth-mile Pro Mod racing. That same year, Musi won the PDRA Rookie of the Year award and got featured on Drag Illustrated Hottest Issue.

However, her achievements do not stop there. In 2015, Lizzy won the second national event in Pro Nitrous with a record-breaking run of 3.79 seconds at 200.48 MPH. That same year, she was introduced to the new Dodge Dart, aka King Kong 6 in Pro Nitrous.
